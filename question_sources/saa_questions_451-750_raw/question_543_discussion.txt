A company runs Amazon EC2 instances in multiple AWS accounts that are individually bled. The company recently purchased a
Savings Pian. Because of changes in the company’s business requirements, the company has decommissioned a large number of
EC2 instances. The company wants to use its Savings Plan discounts on its other AWS accounts.
Which combination of steps will meet these requirements? (Choose two.)
A. From the AWS Account Management Console of the management account, turn on discount sharing from the billing
preferences section. Most Voted
B. From the AWS Account Management Console of the account that purchased the existing Savings Plan, turn on discount
sharing from the billing preferences section. Include all accounts.
C. From the AWS Organizations management account, use AWS Resource Access Manager (AWS RAM) to share the Savings
Plan with other accounts.
D. Create an organization in AWS Organizations in a new payer account. Invite the other AWS accounts to join the organization
from the management account. Most Voted
E. Create an organization in AWS Organizations in the existing AWS account with the existing EC2 instances and Savings Plan.
Invite the other AWS accounts to join the organization from the management account.

Chat says A and E -
A. Correct – You need to enable discount sharing in the management account’s billing preferences.
B. Incorrect – Only the management (payer) account controls discount sharing, not member accounts.
C. Incorrect – Savings Plans cannot be shared via AWS RAM.
D. Creating a new organization is unnecessary; you should use the existing account with the Savings Plan.
E. Correct – You must set up (or use) AWS Organizations in the existing account that owns the Savings Plan, then invite the other accounts to join.
