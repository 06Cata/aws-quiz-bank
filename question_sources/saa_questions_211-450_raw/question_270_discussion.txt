ensure that the data is encrypted at rest before the data is uploaded to the S3 buckets. The data also must be encrypted in transit.
Which solution meets these requirements?

uploads.
D. Enable the security option to encrypt the S3 buckets through the use of a default AWS Key Management Service (AWS KMS)
key.

Neeraja2023 Most Recent 7 months, 1 week ago
Selected Answer: C
C. Create bucket policies that require the use of server-side encryption with S3 managed encryption keys (SSE-S3) for S3 uploads.
Ensures all uploaded objects are encrypted at rest with SSE-S3.
Can also enforce HTTPS (in-transit encryption) by adding a bucket policy denying non-SSL requests (aws:SecureTransport: false).
Enforces encryption automatically and centrally, with minimal operational overhead.
While encryption happens after upload, this is the standard AWS best practice for at-rest encryption and policy enforcement.
Best practical and enforceable approach.

After a little googling, for those who are confused by "at rest":
1. *Encryption at Rest* refers to the encryption applied to the stored data. Encryption may be implemented at the source, where data is generated
and stored at the origin.
2. *Encryption in Transit* refers to encrypting data that is transferred between two nodes of the network.
